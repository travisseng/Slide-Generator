 # _Introduction_
- Brans-Dicke theory is a modification of general relativity that includes a scalar field
- Proposed by Brans and Dicke in 1961 as an alternative to general relativity


---
<style scoped>p,li {font-size:0.92em}</style>

 # Comparison with General Relativity

- Both theories describe the gravitational force as a curvature of spacetime caused by mass-energy
- However, Brans-Dicke theory introduces a scalar field that affects the curvature of spacetime

---
<style scoped>p,li {font-size:0.92em}</style>

 # The Field Equations
- The field equations for Brans-Dicke theory are similar to those of general relativity, but with an additional term involving the scalar field
- The scalar field is assumed to be minimally coupled to matter, meaning its influence on the gravitational force is negligible at low energies


---
<style scoped>p,li {font-size:0.92em}</style>

 # **The Action Principle**

- The action principle for Brans-Dicke theory is similar to that of general relativity, but with an additional term involving the scalar field
- The action principle is used to derive the field equations and describes the evolution of the scalar field and spacetime geometry

 # Notes

- Brans-Dicke theory has been studied extensively in the context of cosmology and has been shown to be able to explain certain features of the cosmic microwave background radiation
- The theory has also been applied to binary pulsars and has been found to be consistent with observational data
- However, Brans-Dicke theory is not without its challenges, including the problem of defining a consistent theory of quantum gravity and the difficulty of reconciling the theory with observational data at high energies.
